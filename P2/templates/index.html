Migration out of LA County

Residents are leaving Los Angeles County more so in the past few years. This analysis explores this concept in-depth to find possible factors that contribute to this observation with the aide of visualizations.

Preliminary Thought Process


Our inital goal was to have an analysis on gentrification throughout the US over a span of 10 years. We wanted to see how rent prices, business growth, and crime rates, changed over time and how this affected the demographic.

Some questions we wanted to answer included:

  • Are different ethnic groups being pushed out of certain metropolitan cities?
  • How do they fluctuate as minorities or whites move in/out of the aforementioned metropolitan areas?
  • How do the businesses differ (categorically) as the demographic changes?
  • Does housing increase as more people move in to a metropolitan area? Additionally, does this lead to the demolition of section 8 housing and displace tenants to a different area?
  • If walking score increases and crime rate decreases as compared to previous years, is this due to gentrification?
  • Are better schools being built once minority residents are forced to move out?

However, the time alloted for the project would not allow us to meet all the requirements and perform such an indepth analysis to answer all the above questions, so we had to narrow down our ideas...


New Scope:

  • Migration into Los Angeles County.
  • How crime rates over time might affect individuals moving into or leaving the county.
  • How individual median income can change over time and perhaps be correlated to the decision to move into or out of LA County.
  • How housing value changes over time
